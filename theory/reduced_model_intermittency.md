# Reduced Three-State Model: Geometry as a Generator of Intermittency

**Status: positive result, numerically verified with two-sided skepticism.**

A minimal stochastic model built from the three strain classes reproduces anomalous scaling of structure functions — multifractal intermittency that K41 does not produce. This positions the geometric classification as a minimal generator of intermittent statistics.

---

## Model

A scalar gradient amplitude $g(t) = e^{\ell(t)}$ evolves under a Markov process over three states matching the strain classes:

- **C_small** (axial stretching): positive drift $a_s > 0$ — vorticity/gradient amplification;
- **C_free** (shear / screening): negative drift $a_f < 0$ — suppression;
- **C_large**: weak drift $a_l \approx 0$ — diffusion.

$$
d\ell = a_{s(t)}\,dt + \sigma_{s(t)}\,dW, \qquad s(t)\in\{\text{small},\text{free},\text{large}\},
$$

with $s(t)$ a continuous-time Markov chain (transition matrix $P$, C_free acting as the transitional hub), and a soft regularization on $\ell$ (viscous analogue).

## Diagnostic

Structure functions of the gradient increments,
$$
S_p(\tau) = \big\langle |g(t+\tau) - g(t)|^p \big\rangle \sim \tau^{\zeta_p},
$$
and the **anomaly indicator** $\zeta_6 - 2\zeta_3$: it is $0$ for K41 (linear $\zeta_p = p/3$) and $<0$ for multifractal intermittency (concave $\zeta_p$).

## Result

Measured exponents (representative run): $\zeta_p = (0.49,\,0.96,\,1.41,\,1.84,\,2.26,\,2.67)$ for $p=1,\dots,6$, clearly concave versus the K41 line $p/3 = (0.33,\dots,2.0)$. The anomaly indicator $\zeta_6 - 2\zeta_3 \approx -0.15$.

**The anomalous scaling is generated by the class structure, not by parameter tuning or by the regularization:**

- **Robust:** across 20 randomized parameter sets (drifts, noises, transition matrix) the indicator is negative in **100%** of cases, range $[-0.28,\,-0.05]$.
- **Significant:** three-state vs single-state, identical regularization, Mann–Whitney $p = 0.0002$; class effect $\Delta(\zeta_6 - 2\zeta_3) \approx -0.09$.
- **Not a regularization artifact:** with no regularization at all, the three-state process gives $\zeta_6 - 2\zeta_3 = -0.11$ while the single-state (Gaussian) process gives $+0.00$ — exactly the K41 value it must give.

The control is decisive: removing the class structure removes the anomaly entirely.

## Interpretation

The three-state geometry is a **minimal generator of intermittency**: the coexistence of *heterogeneous* regimes, modulated by Markovian transitions, produces the heavy tails and concave $\zeta_p$ spectrum that a single homogeneous regime (K41) cannot.

A controlled test isolates the mechanism. The anomaly is **robust to the signs of the per-class drifts** — it persists for the original signs, reversed signs, and restricted-Euler-like signs — and disappears only when all classes are made identical. Removing the drift entirely (classes differing only in noise intensity) still gives a strong anomaly ($\zeta_6-2\zeta_3 \approx -0.20$), whereas differing drifts with equal noise gives a weaker one ($\approx -0.06$). Thus the dominant driver is the **heterogeneity of fluctuation intensity across classes**, not the specific amplify-vs-suppress signs. This is a stronger statement than tying the effect to a particular drift sign, and it does not depend on the (locally underdetermined) sign of the C_small drift.

## Scope and limits

- This is a **reduced model**, not a derivation from Navier–Stokes. It shows the geometric classes are *sufficient* to generate intermittency, not that real turbulence *must* follow this mechanism.
- The drifts/noises are phenomenological and are **not derivable from Navier–Stokes by local class dynamics**: the per-class drift depends on the nonlocal pressure Hessian (restricted-Euler dynamics, which drops pressure, even gives the opposite sign for axial stretching). The result is the **qualitative anomaly and its dependence on class heterogeneity**, not specific exponent values or drift signs.
- Quantitative match to measured turbulence exponents (e.g. She–Lévêque) would require calibration against DNS — out of current scope.

## What this explains that K41 does not

K41 predicts linear $\zeta_p = p/3$ and Gaussian statistics. The three-state model produces concave $\zeta_p$ and non-Gaussian tails from a minimal geometric mechanism, placing intermittency as a direct consequence of the coexistence and alternation of strain regimes.
