# Working Principles

Three operating rules adopted for this program. They define how results are produced, validated, and recorded.

## Rule 1 — Self-refereeing through mandatory skepticism

This program operates without external peer review and without supercomputing validation. The only safeguard against self-deception is disciplined skeptical checking. Every candidate result must pass an explicit falsification test — "could this check disprove the claim?" — *before* it is recorded as a result. A result that cannot be challenged has not been verified.

This discipline is the program's working immunity. Over its development, multiple candidate results were caught and corrected by skeptical checking before they entered the body of established results; in each case a confidence level was lowered, not raised, when a gap was found. Negative results obtained this way are recorded with the same weight as positive ones.

## Rule 2 — Honest decomposition, not disguised downscaling

When a problem exceeds available resources it is decomposed into smaller pieces that fit. Each piece must be a *verifiable* piece — a sub-question that can be genuinely closed — not a reduced-scale computation relabelled as the full result. If a question cannot be settled even after decomposition, it is recorded as open, with the resource requirement stated explicitly (e.g. "requires N ≥ 128 DNS"). This is the line between research and its imitation.

## Rule 3 — Honest record, results into the repository, finished work into papers

Results — large or small, positive or negative — are recorded faithfully in the repository as the development history. Confidence is stated separately for qualitative mechanism and quantitative claim; a single blended percentage that mixes a sound idea with an unproven estimate is avoided. Work that reaches genuine rigor and significance is written up as a paper for journal submission. The repository is the honest ledger; the papers are what the ledger has earned.

## Metric of progress

Progress is measured by the number of *checkable statements closed* — proven **or** refuted — not by the number of iterations or documents produced. A refuted statement is a closed statement. Growth of meta-documentation about the mathematics, in place of new mathematics, is treated as a warning sign, not as progress.
