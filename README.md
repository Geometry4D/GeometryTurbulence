# Geometric Theory of Turbulence

This repository contains ongoing research on a geometric approach to three-dimensional turbulence, based on the concepts of **strain classes**, **two-sided cascades**, and **free zones**.

## Overview

The program develops a coherent geometric framework that aims to explain key phenomena in turbulence:

- Intermittency and anomalous scaling
- Conditional non-uniqueness of weak solutions
- Anomalous dissipation (Onsager-type)
- Lagrangian Coherent Structures as boundaries between geometric regimes

The approach combines rigorous mathematical analysis with reduced-order modeling and potential applications to turbulence parameterization.

## Current Status (June 2026)

- **Phase 0–3 completed**: Formalization of geometric classes, two-sided cascades, geometric flux, and unified mathematical framework.
- **Phase 4 in progress**: Development of reduced geometric models and applications.
- Several articles are in preparation (see `04_Papers/`).

This research builds upon and extends ideas from:
- Geometric theories of turbulence (Constantin)
- Lagrangian Coherent Structures (Haller)
- Convex integration methods for non-uniqueness (Buckmaster, Vicol)
- Multifractal formalism
